Fiction

Sula-Toni Morrison

Toni Morrison’s novel “Sula” is a layered and thought-provoking exploration of themes such as poverty, racism, patriarchy, sexual and economic freedom, and the complexities of human relationships. Set in a small African-American community in Ohio between 1919 and 1965, the novel follows the lives of two childhood friends, Sula Peace and Nel Wright, as they navigate their paths and confront the harsh realities of their world.

1984-George Orwell

George Orwell’s 1984 is a thought-provoking and hauntingly relevant novel that explores the dangers of totalitarianism, government surveillance, and the erosion of individual freedom. The book’s enduring popularity is a testament to its prophetic warnings and its masterful storytelling.Orwell paints an incredibly realistic picture of the consequences of censorship, demonstrating how fascism can start as something subtle before quickly snowballing into totalitarianism. The novel’s depiction of the Ministry of Truth, where history is rewritten and language is manipulated, serves as a chilling reminder of the power of propaganda.

The kite runner-Khaled Hosseini

Khaled Hosseini’s debut novel, The Kite Runner, is a sweeping story of friendship, betrayal, guilt, and redemption set against the devastating backdrop of Afghanistan’s turbulent history. The novel follows the complex and often fraught relationship between Amir, a wealthy boy from Kabul, and Hassan, the son of Amir’s father’s servant. Reviewers have identified guilt and redemption as dominant themes in The Kite Runner. Amir’s childhood failure to save Hassan from abuse and his subsequent guilt haunt him throughout his life, even as he moves to America and builds a new life.

Evenings Weekends

Oisín McKenna’s debut novel, Evenings and Weekends, is a captivating exploration of millennial life in London. Set over two sweltering hot days in June 2019, the story follows a diverse cast of characters as they navigate the challenges of their 30s, including queer identity, financial precarity, and emotional commitment. The novel’s strength lies in its vivid characterizations, with McKenna populating the narrative with fascinating, three-dimensional individuals. The characters’ interconnected lives are woven together seamlessly, capturing the essence of urban life and the city’s ability to forge one’s own identity.

The alchemist-Paulo Coelho

The story follows Santiago, a young Andalusian shepherd, who has recurring dreams of finding treasure at the Pyramids of Egypt. Ignoring the skepticism of his mentor, Santiago sets out on a perilous journey to fulfill his dream. Along the way, he encounters a cast of intriguing characters, including a gypsy woman, a crystal merchant, and an alchemist, each teaching him valuable lessons about listening to his heart, following his dreams, and embracing the universe’s mysteries.
