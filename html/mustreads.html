Must Reads

Blue Sisters

Blue Sisters, written by Coco Mellors, is a poignant and emotional novel that explores the complexities of sisterhood, grief, and individualism. The story revolves around the lives of three sisters, Avery, Bonnie, and Lucky, who reunite in New York a year after the unexpected death of their beloved fourth sister, Nicky.

DreamHouse

Carmen Maria Machado’s “In the Dream House” is a groundbreaking memoir that invents a new form of narrative. The book recounts a psychologically abusive relationship, weaving together academic views on female queerness, play, choose-your-own-adventure elements, and an examination of abuse mechanisms. The reviewer praises Machado’s innovative approach, calling it the most innovative memoir they’ve ever read.

Boulder

Eva Baltasar’s “Boulder” is a poignant and intense novel that explores the complexities of queer love, motherhood, and personal freedom. The story follows a solitary protagonist, Boulder, who abandons her life at sea for a small apartment in Reykjavik with her partner, Samsa. When Samsa decides she wants to have a child, Boulder’s equilibrium is disrupted, and she struggles to reconcile her desires for love and independence.

Evenings Weekends

Oisín McKenna’s debut novel, Evenings and Weekends, is a striking exploration of the struggles and triumphs of young adults navigating love, identity, and financial precarity in contemporary London. The book is a tender and humorous portrayal of the city’s queer community, capturing the essence of urban life and the interconnectedness of its inhabitants.

Happy all the time

“Happy All the Time” is a charming novel by Laurie Colwin, first published in 1978. The book follows the lives of two couples, Guido and Holly, and Vincent and Misty, as they navigate love, relationships, and finding happiness together. With its witty humor, relatable characters, and insightful portrayal of human connections, this novel has become a modern classic.

White Nights

White Nights, a novella by Fyodor Dostoevsky, explores themes of love, loneliness, and the human desire for connection. The tone is melancholic, with a focus on the narrator’s introspective and poetic nature. The story delves into the complexities of the human experience, encouraging readers to reflect on their own experiences with longing and fleeting moments of connection.

The details

Ia Genberg’s “The Details” is a poignant and introspective novel that delves into the intricacies of human relationships and the power of memory. The story revolves around an unnamed narrator, bedridden with a high fever, who becomes obsessed with revisiting a novel from her past, inscribed with a get-well message from her ex-girlfriend Johanna.

Kairos

Jenny Erpenbeck’s novel “Kairos” is a profound and moving portrayal of a tumultuous romance set against the backdrop of East Germany’s collapse in the late 1980s. The story revolves around Katharina, a 19-year-old woman, and Hans, a 53-year-old married writer, whose chance encounter in Berlin sets off a passionate and destructive affair.
